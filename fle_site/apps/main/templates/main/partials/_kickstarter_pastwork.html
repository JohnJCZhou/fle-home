HERE'S WHAT WE'RE GOING TO BUILD

Kolibri will ensure that lack of internet connectivity does not continue to be a barrier to learning. We’ve been listening to our global network of users, and have planned our next stages of development to meet their most urgent needs.

Standalone Android Tablet

The Out-of-the-Box Solution

BARRIER

We support Windows, Linux, and OSX, but low-cost Android tablets depend on nearby servers for connection, meaning they can't be taken home or on the go.

SOLUTION

Kolibri will run as a standalone Android application, and we're working with manufacturers to produce an optimized, preconfigured, low-cost tablet solution, ready to use out of the box.

Learn More

Local Content Curation and Creation

Create Custom Curriculum

BARRIER

Educational content is not "one-size-fits-all." Our partners have consistently demanded the ability to align content to match local curricula, and include locally created content.

SOLUTION

Kolibri will allow organizations and users to upload and organize content into custom "channels." These channels can then be distributed to other Kolibri devices for offline use, creating a better fit for local communities.

LEARN MORE

Peer to Peer Syncing Between Devices

Exchange Content Offline

BARRIER

We already provide ways to leverage intermittent Internet access, but when devices are running in totally offline contexts, sharing and syncing is a cumbersome challenge.

SOLUTION

No internet? No problem. Devices running Kolibri will be able to connect to each other over local wifi or LAN connections to share content, update software, and sync data.

Learn More

Vector Compressed Videos

Download Any Amount of Content

BARRIER

Downloading videos the first time takes an inconveniently long time for those with low bandwidth, and storing huge libraries of video content poses a problem for devices with limited storage.

SOLUTION

We’re developing an innovative tool to compress videos into a very low-bandwidth HTML5 format while retaining quality, so that thousands of videos can fit on a single 4GB device.
